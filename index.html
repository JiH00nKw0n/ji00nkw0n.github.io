I believe that artificial intelligence (AI) can go beyond being a mere tool to promote human freedom and equality while strengthening social solidarity. To realize this vision, AI needs to acquire the ability to understand and reason in a human-like manner. To contribute to making this vision a reality, I study the foundational capabilities that enable human-like understanding and learning.

My research focuses on two complementary capabilities that together enable multimodal understanding and contextual adaptability: aligning vision and language representations and enabling context-driven reasoning.

On the training side, I research methods for better vision–language alignment so that models can perceive and describe the world more like humans. On the inference side, I study how large language models perform in-context learning to adapt to novel information on the fly. Ultimately, my goal is to advance AI systems that can understand and adapt to the world as humans do.
